Dead Sector is a squad-based top-down zombie shooter set in the ruins of a quarantined megacity. Up to four players fight through procedurally influenced city districts — managing ammunition, coordinating flanks, and racing to extract before the horde overwhelms them.

Gameplay

What makes Dead Sector
worth fighting for.

01

Squad Co-op

2–4 player online co-op. Each run rewards coordination over individual skill. A squad that communicates survives. One that doesn't, doesn't.

02

Endless Horde

Wave-based escalation across procedurally influenced city districts. No two runs are identical. The horde adapts to how your squad moves and holds position.

03

Low-Poly Worlds

A deliberate visual identity — clean geometry, dramatic directional lighting, no visual noise. Built to run sharp at high framerates even in peak combat.

04

Gear & Salvage

Between waves, spend salvaged materials on weapons, fortifications, and squad upgrades. Permanent unlocks carry across sessions and reward long-term play.

05

Extraction Runs

Every session ends at an extraction point. Reach it. Hold it. Get out. The last stretch is always the most dangerous — that's intentional.

06

Class System

Four distinct loadout archetypes — Scout, Heavy, Medic, Demo. Each plays differently and builds laterally through mastery, not grinding.

The Setting

Sector 9. Twelve days after the outbreak began, the government sealed the perimeter and declared the eastern districts unsalvageable. Everyone inside was left to manage on their own.

The evacuation that never came. The radio that went silent on day four. Three million people, written off in a single press briefing. The horde didn't wait for permission — and neither did the survivors who refused to become statistics. Dead Sector is set in the weeks that followed: no rescue, no rules, no retreat.
